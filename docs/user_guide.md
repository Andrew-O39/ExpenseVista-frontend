## ExpenseVista Quick Start Guide

If you are new to ExpenseVista, you can get started in just a few minutes. First, create an account or sign in. Next, record at least one income entry and one expense entry so that your dashboard has data to display. After that, create a budget for a category such as groceries, transport, or utilities. Once you have done this, open the Dashboard to review your totals, compare your budget against actual spending, and explore the charts. If needed, you can later export your filtered data as a PDF report or ask the Finance Assistant questions about your spending.

---

## What Is ExpenseVista?

ExpenseVista is a personal finance application that helps you monitor and understand your financial activity. It allows you to create and manage budgets, track income and expenses, compare budgeted amounts against actual spending, and review trends over time. The application also supports PDF exports, multiple currencies, theme selection, onboarding guidance, and an AI-powered Finance Assistant.

ExpenseVista is designed to give individuals, freelancers, and households a clear and practical view of their finances without unnecessary complexity.

---

## Navigating the App

ExpenseVista uses a sidebar layout to make the main areas of the application easy to reach. The sidebar is divided into sections so that navigation remains clear and predictable.

The **Overview** section contains the Dashboard. The **Create** section contains links for recording income, adding expenses, and creating budgets. The **Lists** section contains the Income List, Expense List, and Budget List. The **Account** section contains Logout and Help.

The top-right **User Menu** contains personal settings and utilities such as theme selection, currency settings, onboarding tips, and session information.

---

## Sign In or Register

To begin using ExpenseVista, open the web application and either register for a new account or sign in with an existing one. New users will be asked to verify their email address before continuing. Once verification is complete, you can sign in securely and access the application.

ExpenseVista uses authentication to protect your data and keep your account secure during each session.

---

## Welcome Page

The first time you sign in, ExpenseVista displays a Welcome Page that introduces the main parts of the application. This page explains how to record your first income and expense, how to create budgets, and how to interpret the dashboard and charts.

The Welcome Page is designed as a guided introduction, but it is not limited to first-time use. You can return to it at any time from the **User Menu** by selecting **Onboarding / Welcome Tips**.

---

## User Menu

Your username appears in the top-right corner of the application. Clicking it opens the User Menu, which contains the main personal settings and account-related utilities.

From this menu, you can change the theme, update your currency settings, reopen the onboarding guide, and view session information. The User Menu is intentionally separate from the sidebar so that navigation and personal settings remain clearly distinguished.

---

## Currency and Locale Settings

ExpenseVista supports a wide range of currencies and locale formats. This allows you to display amounts in a way that matches your regional preferences.

To change your settings, open the **User Menu** and choose **Change Currency**. You can then select your preferred currency code, such as EUR, USD, GHS, or INR. You may also choose a locale so that number separators and symbol placement appear correctly. After applying the change, the application updates the displayed monetary values throughout the interface.

These settings remain active until you change them again.

---

## Light and Dark Mode

ExpenseVista supports light mode, dark mode, and an automatic mode that follows your device settings. You can update your preferred theme from the **User Menu**.

The light theme provides a clean, warm interface for regular daytime use, while the dark theme offers a more subdued interface for lower-light environments. Auto mode allows the application to follow your system-wide preference.

Your selected theme is remembered between sessions.

---

## Dashboard Overview

The Dashboard provides a high-level view of your finances. It is intended to help you understand your financial position at a glance before exploring the more detailed list pages.

At the top of the Dashboard, you will see key figures such as **Total Income**, **Total Expenses**, and **Net Balance**. Below that, you can review visual sections such as **Spending by Category**, **Budgets vs Actual Spending**, and **Income vs Expenses Over Time**.

The Dashboard also supports filters, allowing you to adjust the period or category being analyzed. Some sections may be collapsible so that you can focus on the information most relevant to you.

---

## Managing Budgets

Budgets help you plan your spending and monitor how closely your actual expenses follow your targets. In ExpenseVista, budgets are created by category and period, which means you can define limits for weekly, monthly, quarterly, or yearly use.

Once a budget is created, ExpenseVista compares your spending against that amount and highlights whether you are within budget, close to the limit, or over budget. This information appears both in the Dashboard and in the Budget List.

Budgets can also be edited or deleted later if your needs change.

---

## Adding and Tracking Expenses

Expense tracking is one of the core features of ExpenseVista. Recording expenses regularly gives the app the information it needs to generate accurate summaries, reports, and comparisons against your budgets.

Expenses are linked to categories, which makes it easier to understand where your money is going over time. Optional descriptions and notes allow you to capture extra context for later review.

---

## Add an Expense

To record a new expense, open the sidebar and choose **Add Expense**. Enter the category, amount, and any optional description or notes. Once saved, the expense will appear in the Expense List and will also be reflected in relevant charts and budget comparisons.

If you are unsure which category to use, ExpenseVista can provide an AI-assisted category suggestion based on the description you enter.

---

## Recording Income

Income entries help complete the financial picture shown throughout the application. By recording salary payments, freelance income, side income, or other earnings, you make it possible for ExpenseVista to compare income against expenses and calculate your net balance.

Income entries also contribute to the Dashboard charts and list-page reports.

---

## Add Income

To add income, open the sidebar and select **Record Income**. Enter the amount, category, source, and date received. After saving, the entry will appear in the Income List and update the Dashboard figures and charts.

Consistently recording income makes the trend and net balance sections more meaningful over time.

---

## List Pages

ExpenseVista includes dedicated list pages for **Income**, **Expenses**, and **Budgets**. These pages are useful when you want to move beyond the Dashboard and inspect detailed records.

Each list page includes filters, search tools, totals for the current view, and row-level actions such as edit or delete. On smaller screens, the tables are designed to remain accessible by allowing horizontal scrolling where needed.

The list pages are also where most users will export filtered reports.

---

## Exporting Reports as PDF

ExpenseVista allows you to export your filtered financial data as PDF reports. This is useful when you want to keep a snapshot of your current view, share a summary, or archive a report for later reference.

You can export PDFs from the **Income List**, **Expense List**, **Budget List**, and the **Budgets vs Actual Spending** table on the Dashboard. Each export includes the currently loaded and currently filtered data shown in the application, rather than every possible row across all pages.

A typical export includes the report title, export date and time, the active filters, the visible table rows, and a totals summary where relevant.

To export, open the relevant page or dashboard section and click **Export PDF**.

---

## Email Notifications

ExpenseVista sends automated emails for certain important events. These include email verification, password reset flows, and budget alert notifications when your spending reaches important thresholds.

Budget alerts can help you stay aware of how close you are to exceeding a limit. Depending on your configuration, you may receive notifications as spending approaches or exceeds the allowed amount.

These messages are intended to support awareness and help you act before overspending becomes a larger issue.

---

## Finance Assistant

The Finance Assistant is available as a floating chat bubble in the lower-right corner of the application. It allows you to ask natural-language questions about your financial data and receive immediate insight.

For example, you can ask how much you spent this month, whether you are over budget in a particular category, or how your income compares to your expenses over a specific period. The assistant can also help interpret your financial activity and suggest category usage in some contexts.

The assistant is meant to make financial exploration quicker and more conversational, especially when you do not want to manually inspect each list or chart.

## Additional Features

ExpenseVista includes several supporting features that improve the overall experience.

### Session Management

For security, user sessions expire after a fixed period of time. Before expiry, ExpenseVista warns you so that you can continue your work or log in again if needed. Session information can be viewed from the **User Menu**.

### Multi-Currency Support

All relevant financial values update based on your selected currency and locale settings. This affects budgets, expenses, income, and exported totals.

### Help and Documentation

The application includes an in-app **Help** page that renders the user guide directly inside the application. This allows users to read documentation without leaving ExpenseVista.

## FAQ

### Why do my totals differ from my bank account?

ExpenseVista only reflects the data that you enter into the application. If an entry is missing, the totals will not match your actual financial records.

### Can I track multiple bank accounts?

At the moment, ExpenseVista does not provide separate multi-account tracking. Future versions may support multiple wallets, accounts, or profiles.

### How do I reset my password?

Use the **Forgot Password** option on the login screen and follow the instructions sent to your email address.

### Why does my session expire?

Sessions expire automatically for security reasons. This helps protect your account if the app is left open on a shared or unattended device.

## Troubleshooting

| Issue | Possible Fix |
|------|--------------|
| The Finance Assistant is not responding | Make sure you are signed in and connected to the internet. |
| Verification or reset emails are not arriving | Check your spam or junk folder and confirm that your email address is correct. |
| Budget alerts are missing | Ensure your email is verified and that spending has crossed the configured threshold. |
| Theme changes are not visible | Refresh the page after changing the theme if needed. |
| Currency is not updating | Make sure you clicked Apply after selecting the new currency. |
| A table is hard to read on a smaller screen | Scroll horizontally to view all columns. |

## Smart Budgeting Tips

A good budgeting strategy is not only about setting limits, but also about reviewing patterns consistently. Start with realistic values based on your recent spending instead of ideal targets that are difficult to maintain. Review your highest-spending categories regularly and adjust budgets when your habits change.

ExpenseVista works best when you use it consistently. Recording income and expenses regularly, reviewing the Dashboard, and asking the Finance Assistant questions can help you spot trends earlier and make better financial decisions.
